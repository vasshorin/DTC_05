Restrictions for Travel to (Canada)

Restrictions:

  1. Entering Nunavut as a fully vaccinated travellers

  2. Self-isolation and other requirements



Proof of vanccination

Fully vaccinated travellers:
  • Proof of vaccination certificate or PVC and,
  • Nunavut CPHO travel authorization letter.

As a reminder, PVC letters can be obtained by contacting your local health centre. For vaccine exemption application process and to obtain a CPHO travel authorization letter, please go to: Vaccinated travellers.


*More details can be found at Proof of Vaccinaation Guidelines.

Self-isolation requirements

All partially vaccinated or unvaccinated travellers:

All partially vaccinated or unvaccinated travellers (other than dependents traveling on a vaccinated parent or guardian exemption, and medical travellers on the EMTI program):

  • Nunavut CPHO travel authorization proving completion of 14-day isolation in designated GN isolation site.

All partially vaccinated and unvaccinated EMTI program travellers:

  • Nunavut CPHO travel authorization proving completion of 14-day isolation in designated GN isolation site, and
  • A negative COVID-19 rapid molecular test taken within 72 hours of departure.

For more information on the EMTI program see Expedited Medical Travel Isolation (EMTI).


Isolation process:
  • Seven days prior to the isolation check-in date, travellers must complete the Pre-Isolation Check-In form and submit it to nuisolationreservations@nunavutcare.ca, as soon as possible.
  • Travellers will receive an email confirming receipt of their Pre-Isolation Check-In form and an Isolation Information Package.
  • At the end of the 14 days, residents will be cleared and provided a letter signed by the Chief Public Health Officer allowing them to return to their home community.

*More details can be found at Travel and Isolation.

Mask requirements

Customers who do not have a mandatory face covering will not be allowed to travel.

Masks must be made from at least two layers of tightly woven fabric, must be clean and dry, and must fully cover the nose and mouth.

Please note that the following masks that will not be accepted, as they do not limit the spread of COVID-19:

  • Non-medical masks with an exhalation valve or vent.
  • Non-medical masks made with mesh or lace fabric.
  • Masks that are moist, dirty or damaged.
  • Neck gaiters.
  • Bandanas.
  • Face shields.

*More details can be found at Face covering requirements for air travellers.
