Restrictions for Travel to (Canada)

Restrictions:

  1. Entering Alberta as a fully vaccinated travellers

  2. Self-isolation and other requirements



Proof of vanccination

As of November 15, 2021, Albertans 12 and older can only show their proof of vaccination through an Alberta vaccine record QR code. Visitors from other provinces can show their proof of vaccination in whatever form their home province has issued. The following businesses will be required to check for proof of vaccine or a privately paid negative COVID test (within 72 hours) for customers over the age of 12:

  • Restaurants (for sit-down dining);
  • Entertainment and recreation centres (bowling, racing entertainment, arcades, billiards halls, other similar venues);
  • Movie theatres;
  • Private social events held in public facilities;
  • Amenities in hotels (pools, gyms, etc.);
  • Conferences, meeting spaces, halls and rented spaces;
  • Weddings and funerals held in public venues;

*More details can be found at COVID-19 Traveller Updates.

Self-isolation requirements

If you qualify as a fully vaccinated traveller, no further travel restrictions apply. No quarantine required.

*More details can be found at COVID-19 travel requirements.

Mask requirements

Masks are mandatory:
  • in all indoor public spaces, workplaces and places of worship, including eligible businesses that implement the Restriction Exemption Program
  • for employees in all indoor work settings, except while alone in work station
  • for all students in Grades 4 and up, plus staff and teachers in all grades

*More details can be found at COVID-19: Mask requirements.
