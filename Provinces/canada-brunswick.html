Restrictions for Travel to (Canada)

Restrictions:

  1. Entering New Brunswick as a fully vaccinated travellers

  2. Self-isolation and other requirements



Proof of vanccination

Fully vaccinated: A person is considered to be fully vaccinated when at least two weeks has passed since their final dose of a Health Canada approved COVID-19 vaccine.

Partially vaccinated: A person is considered to be partially vaccinated when at least two weeks have passed since their first dose of a Health Canada approved COVID-19 vaccine. This only applies to vaccines that consist of two doses.

Fully vaccinated travellers are required to upload proof of vaccination to the Travel Form. People who do not upload appropriate proof will be required to follow the rules for unvaccinated travellers.


*More details can be found at Travelling to Newfoundland and Labrador.

Self-isolation requirements

Fully vaccinated travellers are not required to self-isolate or be tested for COVID-19.

Partially vaccinated and unvaccinated travellers are required to:

  • Fully self-isolate until they receive negative results from a test taken on day 7 or later. If the traveller chooses not to have a test, they must self-isolate for 14 days.
  • After receipt of negative test results on day 7 or later, and until the full 14 days of a self-isolation period has passed, partially vaccinated and unvaccinated travellers, as well as any children that travelled with them or have isolated with them, must:
    • Avoid vulnerable people, long term care and personal care homes.
    • Not attend daycare, camp, sporting events, and must avoid large crowded settings.
    • After receipt of negative test results, children can attend school but must otherwise limit contact with others as described above and cannot attend before or after school programs.
    • Those younger than 5 are not required to be tested as long as the parents have their test on day 7 or later but are still required to abide by these limitations after the parents receive a negative test.
*More details can be found at Travelling to Newfoundland and Labrador.

Mask requirements

Effective September 22 Masks will be mandatory in all indoor public spaces, including:
  • public spaces where the public and employees interact, such as retail businesses, malls, service centres, places of worship, and restaurants and bars except while eating;
  • organized indoor gatherings in public spaces, such as weddings and funerals;
  • common areas like lobbies, elevators and hallways, and public shared spaces including those in private sector and government workspaces; and
  • public transportation.

*More details can be found at Masks - Government of New Brunswick.
